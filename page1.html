What is Pen and Paper Roleplay?

Pen and Paper Roleplay is a type of game genre where you normally sit around a table with other people and act out stories imagined by everyone together. If that sounds weird to you let me explain further.



Roleplay

So the roleplay aspect of the game is very straightforward, you have a character and you act as if you were that character. you might be a wise wizard, a genius hacker, or just a strong warrior of any kind, regardless what your character is you give him a personality. This personality might be completely different from what you expect or exactly what you would think it is, but it is important to remember that every character is different in some way and you as a player need to act out these differences somehow. So if you play a sly thief you might feel reluctant to talk since you like the whole silent but deadly robber aesthetic or you can play that as an outgoing con artist who tricks people out of their money with clever word play. Since you have to act out what you want to do you might think one is more difficult than the other since the first one doesnt really speak, but fear not more often than not you will be rewarded by the gamemaster for clever roleplaying. What a gamemaster is will be explained later.



Pen and Paper

The pen and paper aspect is just representing the medium the game is played in. Imagining cool battles is great and all but without rules it would just be a power-fantasy of some friends. These games follow a system of rules which determine how you can interact with the world the gamemaster provides you with. Each system normally gives you some sort of stats which show a character's ability in one field of expertise and you write these stats down on a piece of paper with a pen -see where this is going?- Part of the rules are usually a set of dice which determine success of actions in some way. Using the rules and your imagination in tandem you can experience all kinds of stories and have fun with your friends or soon to be friends, because let's be honest here this game isn't your run of the mill 1 hour board game no. I would say it is at least 3 hours per session and you would probably play once a week to get the story moving forward. Yeah it is very time consuming.



Gamemaster

So "gamemaster" what is that? Usually these kinds of games have someone who governs the world, who guides the players through the world, who plays the other people in the world. Said someone is usually known as the gamemaster. The gamemaster's "job" is to provide a fun narrative and enough improvisation skills to let the players play the game. This is an important fact about this genre, there are not many systems that don't utilize a gamemaster of some sort so if you want to play the game and have no one to master well then you should probably face the facts that you either find some one who would like to master or you learn to master yourself.



Summary

To summarize the genre is a combination of ruled improvized acting and a pinch of luck with the dice rolls. If you take the time to set up a group and play regularly you will have lots of fun and might even improve on some social skills, who knows.

How can I get started?

You are going to need the following:

  • a Rulebook for the game you want to play
  • some multi sided dice (in most Systems)
    • 4 sided
    • 6 sided
    • 8 sided
    • 10 sided
    • 12 sided
    • 20 sided
  • some friends who also want to play with you
  • willingness to act out your character
  • and general fun

If you don't have the opportunity to get a Rulebook there are websites for some games.
